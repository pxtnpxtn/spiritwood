Our Operation
Trees
The plantations have been carefully mapped and measured to ensure the perfect balance between the fast growing gmelina tree, while also having enough of the slower growing mohogany.
White Teak (Gmelina Arborea)
Gmenlina is a fast growing, moderately sized deciduous tree. From a seedling to an adult in X years, it averages Y meters of growth per year, as it grows to a maximum of 30 meters high. The straight, cylindrical bole is commonly around 50cm in diameter, however some specimens are in excess of 140cm.
Mohogany (Swietenia Macrophylla)
The mohogany tree is one of the non-indigenous trees recommended for reforestation by the Philippine forestry authorities. The giant mohogany is a slow growing evergreen tree. It reaches a height of 30 to 40 meters, with some specimens reaching up to 60 meters tall. With a straight and cylindrical bole, firm lumber and a diameter of 100 to 120 centimetres, it’s no wonder that mohogany is regarded as the world’s finest timber for high-class furniture, instruments and cabinet work.
Planting & Husbandry
For more than 20 years, we have been planting and managing our expanding tree farms. Once the seedlings have grown to an appropriate size, they are transplanted into the plantations. They are then overseen by our foresters and forest guards that perform maintenance and husbandry work, ensuring that the trees are kept safe and secure.
Sustainability is at the core of Spiritwood. We believe in harvesting responsibly, following best practices such as selective cutting and replanting, where new growth will replace the trees being cut, allowing for long term stable production.
The trees that were planted in the early years of the company are now ready to support commercial selective harvesting, with our plantations in San Pascual and Magsaysay being the first to be brought into production.
Lumber Milling
With such careful attention being put on how we grow our trees and selecting which to cut, comes the benefit of having our lumber mill operating regularly. Our clients range from local businesses to the government.
One project we are very proud of is building and delivering over 36,000 desks to the Department of Education for local schools.
The company began harvesting in the last quarter of 2018, bringing the mill into commercial operation. The mill is able to handle more than three million board feet of rough cut lumber per year.
